# Month Overview

The month view is the long lens. It is the view you reach for when you are booking a dentist appointment and need to find a free Tuesday, or when you are trying to remember which weekend the school fair falls on. A traditional grid, drawn with the same paper-on-the-wall aesthetic as everything else in Nesta.

Each day in the grid carries small coloured dots — one for each event or task scheduled. Busy days are obvious at a glance. Empty days stand out just as clearly. It is the kind of view that belongs on a kitchen wall, and that is exactly what it feels like.

## Key Features

- Traditional month grid with clean serif date numbers
- Coloured dot indicators showing event density per day
- Today is gently highlighted with a subtle circle
- Tap any day to jump to its full day view
- Swipe between months with smooth page transitions

## How It Works

The month grid fills the screen with seven columns and five or six rows, depending on the month. Each cell displays the date number and up to four coloured dots beneath it. The dots correspond to family members' events — a blue dot for Dad, a pink dot for Emma — so you can see at a glance whose month is fullest.

If a day has more than four events, a small "+2" indicator appears, letting you know there is more to see without cluttering the grid. Tap any day to drop into the full day view. Swipe left to advance to the next month, right to go back.

The month view deliberately avoids trying to show event titles or times in the grid. That level of detail belongs in the day view. Here, the goal is purely spatial orientation: where is the family busy, where is there breathing room, and what does the shape of the month look like?
