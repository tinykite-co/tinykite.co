# Display Mode — Dedicated Family Display

Turn any tablet or old phone into a beautiful, always-on family command centre. Display Mode transforms Nesta into a glanceable schedule that the whole household can read from across the room — mounted on a kitchen wall, propped on a shelf, or set on the counter next to the coffee machine.

This is the feature that brings Nesta closest to its inspiration: an actual paper calendar on the wall. Display Mode locks the device into a focused, large-type view that auto-rotates between today's timeline and the week ahead. No one can accidentally swipe into YouTube or change the settings. It just sits there, quietly showing your family what is happening, like a thoughtful piece of furniture.

## Key Features

- Full-screen, always-on display with automatic screen wake
- Large serif typography readable from three metres away
- Auto-rotation between today's timeline and the upcoming week
- Child-safe lock — no access to settings, other apps, or the home screen
- Automatic night dimming based on your household's bedtime schedule

## How It Works

To enter Display Mode, open Nesta's settings and tap "Display Mode." Choose your rotation interval — how long to show each view before transitioning — and tap "Start." The device immediately enters a full-screen, locked display. The status bar disappears, navigation buttons are hidden, and Nesta fills every pixel.

The display cycles between two views. First, today's timeline: the same day view you know, but scaled up with larger fonts, bolder colours, and higher contrast for distance readability. Second, the week ahead: a simplified strip showing one line per day with the most important events. The transitions between views are slow and gentle — no jarring cuts.

To exit Display Mode, tap and hold the bottom-right corner for three seconds. A PIN prompt appears if you have set one, ensuring that curious little fingers cannot accidentally leave the display. This makes it genuinely safe to use Display Mode on a device accessible to children — they can look, but they cannot wander.

## Setting Up Your Family Display

The best setup is a dedicated device that does nothing else. An old tablet is perfect. Mount it on the kitchen wall at eye level, plug it into power, enable Display Mode, and forget about it. The display updates itself over WiFi as family members add and complete events from their own devices. It is a living, breathing family schedule that requires zero maintenance.
